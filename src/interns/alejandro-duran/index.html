About Me

I graduated from USC in 2018 with a bachelors in chemistry. Before committing the next 12 years of my life to medicine, I decided to travel and teach in China. I spent one month in Shanghai and two months in Beijing. It was life changing. I realized that I was a lot more passionate about meeting and connecting with people from all over the world than I was about studying science.

I worked odd jobs for a couple years. At a friend's suggestion, I picked up coding during last year's Covid lock-down and began self-teaching. I was surprised how much I enjoyed it. Math, physics, and science have always been my strength, and the logical, technical side of programming just fits me naturally. In addition to enjoying problem-solving and technical challenges, I value the freedom it gives me: being able to work from anywhere around the world with all kinds of people is the key reason I dropped medicine and embraced programming.

I would love to learn more about back-end systems during my internship. I am familiar enough with front-end to know my way around it. I want to develop my back-end chops and gain a fuller understanding of APIs, databases, and networks. Please check out my project protfolio and my GitHub profile!
